Komodo Dragon (Varanus komodoensis)

Where They Chill:
Komodo dragons are native to a few islands in Indonesia like Komodo, Rinca, Flores, and Gili Motang. They’re into hot, dry places with some grass and low trees.

What They Eat:
These guys are serious carnivores. They munch on deer, wild pigs, and sometimes even water buffalo. They’ve got a killer instinct and won’t shy away from eating smaller dragons or carrion if they get the chance.

How Long They Live:
In the wild, Komodo dragons can stick around for about 30 years. They can hit the same age in captivity if they’re well taken care of.

Size and Looks:
Komodo dragons are the biggest lizards out there. They can grow up to 10 feet long and weigh between 150 to 200 pounds. Males are usually bigger than females. They’ve got a muscular build, sharp claws, and a nasty set of teeth.

Behavior:
These lizards are pretty solo. They come together only to mate or feast. They’re aggressive and territorial. They hunt by sneaking up on prey and then going for the kill with a powerful bite.

Reproduction:
Komodo dragons mate between May and August. Females lay 15 to 30 eggs, which she buries in a nest. After about 8 to 9 months, the eggs hatch, and the little ones are on their own right away. They climb trees to stay safe from predators, including big Komodo dragons.

Conservation Status:
Komodo dragons are considered Vulnerable, which means they’re at risk of becoming endangered. Their biggest threats are habitat loss and climate change. Conservation efforts are ongoing to help keep their population stable.

Komodo Dragon Venom

Komodo dragons have venom, but it’s not like snake venom. Their bite introduces harmful bacteria that cause severe infections. This bacterial infection weakens their prey, making it easier for the Komodo dragon to catch and kill.
